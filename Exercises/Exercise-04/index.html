We

arrived

just

before the

sunset.

The air

was warm,

but the sand

felt cool

beneath our feet.

The

ocean itself

was

so calm,

but

the wind

was

strongly blowing.

Waves

gently touched

the shore,

then pulled

back again

like whispers

fading into silence.

Footsteps

disappeared

in

the wet and soft

sand

as

we

walked together.

The scent

of salt

and seaweed

filled the air.

A distant call

from a seagull

echoed above.

Somewhere

far

away,

a boat

was drifting

slowly.

Its sail

barely moved,

the water

holding it

in a gentle

embrace.

The sky

turned

from orange

to deep purple.

We

stood

still.

Listening.

Feeling.

The waves

spoke

in quiet murmurs.

The sky

dimmed

as the first stars

began

to shine.

And we

simply

existed,

in that moment,

together.
